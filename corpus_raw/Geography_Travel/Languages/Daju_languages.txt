Daju languages,  group of related languages scattered across the Nuba Hills of southern Sudan (including Lagowa, Liguri, and Shatt), western Sudan (including Bego, Geneina, Daju of Darfur [also called Nyala], and Nyalgulgule), and eastern Chad (including Dar Sila and Dar Daju). The Daju languages are classified in the Eastern Sudanic group of the Nilo-Saharan language family.
