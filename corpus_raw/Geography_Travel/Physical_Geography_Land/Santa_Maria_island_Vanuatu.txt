Santa Maria, also called Gaua,  largest of the Banks Islands in Vanuatu, southwestern Pacific Ocean. The island, with an area of 132 square miles (342 square km), is rugged and rises to Garet, an active volcano (2,615 feet [797 metres]) that contains a lake in its caldera at 1,404 feet (428 metres). The volcano has had several minor eruptions since 1962. The mostly Melanesian inhabitants produce copra and cacao for export.
