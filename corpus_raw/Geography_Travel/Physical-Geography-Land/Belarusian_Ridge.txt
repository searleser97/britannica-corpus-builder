Belarusian Ridge,  upland region in Belarus. From northeastern Poland the ridge runs southeast into western Belarus and then swings northeast. Its total length is 320 miles (520 km). The ridge, covered by marine sands and clays, is in reality a series of separate uplands, of which the highest point is Dzyarzhynskaya Hill, elevation 1,135 feet (346 metres), in the Minsk Upland. To the south of the ridge lie the extensive Pripet Marshes.
