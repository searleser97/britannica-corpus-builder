Alfonso Ferrabosco, II,  (born c. 1575, Greenwich, London, Eng.â€”died  March 1628, Greenwich), English composer, viol player, and lutenist, known especially for his music for viol. The illegitimate son of the composer Alfonso Ferrabosco I, he was educated in music at the expense of Queen Elizabeth I and remained in royal service until his death. He collaborated with Ben Jonson and the architect Inigo Jones in the extravagant masques produced at the court of James I. His fantasies and pavanes for viols are among the finest of their kind. His Lessons for 1, 2 and 3 Viols and his Ayres for voice and lute were published in 1609.
