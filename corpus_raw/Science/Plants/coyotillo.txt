Coyotillo,   (Karwinskia humboldtiana), woody shrub of the buckthorn family (Rhamnaceae) that is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It grows about 1–7 m (3–23 feet) tall and has opposite, oval leaves 2.5–7.5 cm (1–3 inches) long. The small, greenish flowers, which grow in clusters, are followed by brownish black oval berries about 1 cm in diameter. The fruit is poisonous to humans and to livestock.
