It grows to 1.5 metres (5 feet) tall and has bristly, divided leaves. It bears umbels (flat-topped clusters) of white or pink flowers with a single dark purple flower in the centre that produce ribbed fruits with sharp spines. The enlarged root is edible but acrid. Native to Eurasia, it now has a nearly cosmopolitan range and is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental.
