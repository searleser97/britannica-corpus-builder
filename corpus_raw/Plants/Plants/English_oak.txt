English oak,  (Quercus robur), also called brown oak,  ornamental and timber tree of the beech family (Fagaceae) that is native to Eurasia but also cultivated in North America and Australia. The tree has a short stout trunk with wide-spreading branches and may grow to a height of 25 metres (82  feet). The short-stalked leaves, 13 cm (5 inches) long or longer, have three to seven pairs of rounded lobes; they are dark green above and pale green beneath and retain their colour into winter.
